What is Mounjaro?

Mounjaro is an injectable prescription that may help adults and children with obesity, or some overweight adults and children who also have weight-related medical problems, to lose excess body weight and keep the weight off.

What are potential side-effects?

Everyone can have a different experience, but here are the most common side effects experienced by patients taking Mounjaro: nausea; diarrhea; decreased appetite; vomiting; constipation; indigestion; stomach pain.

How do I use it?

Take Mounjaro once a week, at any time of the day. You'll start with 2.5mg, and after 4 weeks you'll move up to 5mg.

Lose up to 25% body weight

Mounjaro has been found to be very effective at supporting substantial weight loss. Clinical trials show 1 in 3 people using Mounjaro 10mg or 15mg can lose up to 25% of their body weight. The studies also found that Mounjaro works best when used alongside changes to diet and increased physical exercise.

The science behind Mounjaro

Mounjaro works by activating receptors of 2 hormones in the body, called GLP-1 and GIP receptors, which are mainly found in the pancreas, brain, stomach, and other organs.

Mounjaro works in multiple ways. It helps:

  • The body release insulin when blood sugar is high.
  • The body remove excess sugar from the blood.
  • Stop the liver from making and releasing too much sugar.
  • Reduce how much food is eaten.
  • Slow down how quickly food leaves the stomach. This lessens over time.
